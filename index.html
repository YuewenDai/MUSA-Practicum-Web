INTRO TO BACKGROUND

Our project uses the 1-meter resolution land cover raster data, which is 900 times more detailed than the commonly used NLCD data for prediction. This unique dataset helps us create a more precise tool to help the local government visualize the potential of land cover change in a specific area more directly.

The machine learning model is built based on the land cover data from three counties that have varied patterns of settlement and ecosystem, so it is generalizable to the county level. It can be used broadly, including land use planning, architecture construction, and landscape design.

By predicting area shift from pervious to impervious surface, our tool helps provide an opportunity to track how those areas might be influenced by climate risks, such as sea level rise and urban heat island effect. It helps the planning departments identify the areas where interventions(growing tree canopy, restoration of natural habitats, etc.) may be needed to mitigate the impacts of climate change.
